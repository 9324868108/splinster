ACCOUNTABILITY

Freelancer Accountability That Protects Your Guarantee

Splinster is an insurance-backed delivery service. To keep trust high for clients—and fair for reliable freelancers—we enforce strict commitment rules. We do not entertain abandonment, silence, or repeated low-quality delivery.

How we work (clear rules, fair enforcement)

When a freelancer accepts a project, they commit to completing all milestones, meeting deadlines, and communicating proactively. This is non-negotiable—because our guarantee depends on it.

Communication requirement
  • ✓Projects > $5,000: daily status update required
  • ✓Projects < $5,000: update at least every 2 days
  • ✓Extended silence can be treated as abandonment
Fairness protections (we’re strict, not unfair)
  • •24-hour cooling-off after acceptance: decline with zero penalty
  • •Valid emergencies (with proof) can be exempt from penalties
  • •Dispute window: penalties can be disputed within 7 days
Note: This is a simplified summary. Final enforcement is governed by the freelancer agreement and project acceptance timestamp.

Abandonment penalties (tiered)

Abandoning a project without a valid emergency triggers a penalty on the value of completed work. Repeat behavior escalates quickly.

Tier 1 — First offense (30-day window)
15% penalty
Formal warning + replacement cost coverage.
Tier 2 — Second offense (30-day window)
30% penalty
Account flagged as high-risk.
Tier 3 — Third offense (30-day window)
50% penalty
30-day suspension (no new projects).
Tier 4 — Pattern of abandonment (60-day window)
100% penalty
Permanent ban. We protect the guarantee above all.
Example (partial completion abandonment)

Project value: $1,000. Freelancer completes $500 of work, then abandons. On a first offense: 15% penalty = $75.
Freelancer receives: $425. Splinster retains $75 to fund a fast replacement.

What counts as a valid emergency? +

We understand life happens. With timely communication and proof within 7 days, penalties may be waived for:

  • ✓Medical hospitalization / serious illness (doctor or hospital proof)
  • ✓Immediate family emergency (appropriate documentation)
  • ✓Force majeure (region-wide outage/disaster with official proof)

Not valid: “busy with other work”, “better paying project”, vacation without pre-approval, lost motivation.

Penalties are allocated to replacement incentives, operations/monitoring, client compensation, and insurance reserves—so delivery stays protected.
